Signal Guide — What Each Layer Means

■ Silence (librosa)

Blue regions = gaps where audio drops below threshold for ≥0.3s. Natural pause points — ideal places to cut clips without chopping mid-word.

■ Energy (librosa)

RMS loudness per 10s window, normalized 0–1. High energy = raised voices, audience reactions, or intense exchanges. Doesn't distinguish excited speech from background noise — just measures volume.

■ Pitch Variance (librosa pyin)

Standard deviation of vocal pitch (F0) per 10s window. High = animated, expressive speech (questions, emphasis, surprise). Low = monotone. Best combined with energy: high energy + high pitch variance ≈ compelling content.

■ Spectral Contrast (librosa)

Peak-to-valley difference in frequency spectrum. High = clean speech; low = noise/applause/music. Stays flat for studio recordings (like these videos) — more useful for live events or mixed-audio content.

| Scene Cuts (PySceneDetect)

Frame-to-frame color histogram comparison. Catches hard cuts and jump cuts at frame-level precision. Misses smooth cross-dissolves (the per-frame change is too gradual to trigger).

Pitch variance and energy

Pitch variance and energy both show meaningful variation across these videos, so they appear directionally promising as signals.

That said, they likely need further processing before they become genuinely useful for highlight identification on their own.

Spectral contrast

Spectral contrast is nearly flat across all four videos because they're all studio recordings with consistent audio quality. It would become useful for videos with mixed audio environments (live events, outdoor shoots, audience Q&A segments).

Silence-based clip boundary snapping

Silence is already proving useful for clip boundary quality. In the latest commit, we decided to leverage transcript-detected silence to normalize clip start and end boundaries, which helps avoid abrupt mid-sentence cutoffs.

Librosa-based silence detection (visible as blue regions on the timeline) still looks promising, but we should further evaluate whether it is worth incorporating beyond the current transcript-based approach.
